# Dataset composition and provenance

What the published Hugging Face dataset contains, and how each part was vetted.
Generated by `eval/hf_export/` (spec [hf-dataset-export](../specs/hf-dataset-export.md));
live figures in `data/hf-dataset/stats.json`. Snapshot: 28.57 h, 36,846 rows,
metadata-only (audio is fetched on demand with `scripts/fetch_clip.py`).

## The three pieces (28.57 h)

| Piece | rows | hours | train / val | how it was vetted |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Corrections (hand-picked) | 5,054 | 4.71 h | 4.00 / 0.72 | full human review, plus an explicit *include* decision |
| Corrections (NB2 leftover) | 12,032 | 8.91 h | 7.16 / 1.75 | automated pipeline: filters, LLM judge, partial audio check |
| Backbone (no-edit) | 19,760 | 14.94 h | 11.32 / 3.62 | meeting-trust plus alignment |
| Total | 36,846 | 28.57 h | 22.47 / 6.10 | and every row is force-aligned to its audio |

Signal (corrections) is 13.63 h (48%). Plain speech (backbone) is 14.94 h (52%).

## 1. Hand-picked corrections, 4.71 h

The strongest part of the set. A reviewer opened each utterance in the review UI,
saw the `before`/`after` diff and the audio, made or approved the correction, and
marked it *include* (worth training on). That is real per-utterance human review,
plus our own decision to keep it.

The raw included spans came to 7.42 h. The alignment gate then removed one
utterance whose timestamp was broken, a 2.7-hour span from a data error, which
leaves 4.71 h of real content.

## 2. NB2 leftover corrections, 8.91 h

Corrections chosen by the automated "next-batch" (NB2) selection pipeline, not
picked by us one at a time. How NB2 was built (`data/next-batch/`):

1. Start: 93,584 candidate edits (every user or task edit in the corpus, minus
   the ones already curated).
2. Hard prefilter on duration, word-diff, edit-chain, and CER-proxy bounds:
   61,730 survivors.
3. Greedy-diverse shortlist, balanced across error categories, clip lengths, and
   cities: 25,000.
4. LLM judge in two tiers (keep, reject, unsure): 13,377 kept, 8,953 rejected,
   2,636 unsure.
5. Audio check on a city-balanced subset (5,011, capped at 600 per city, val
   cities excluded): re-transcribed and compared for faithfulness at
   `cer_local ≤ 0.2`, leaving 4,329 confirmed faithful.

Who originally made these edits, out of the 12,032 we use: 26% were human-made
(`user_only`) and 74% were LLM-proposed and then touched by a human
(`task_then_user`), with a handful mixed. So they carry human signal, but a
pipeline selected and vetted them rather than a person reviewing each one like in
piece 1.

## 3. No-edit backbone, 14.94 h

Untouched ASR utterances from meetings a reviewer worked on but left alone.
Vetting is meeting-level trust (`frac_user ≥ 0.15`, or the `humanReview` flag,
minus the denylist) plus the alignment gate. Call it "alignment-passed no-edit
ASR": a minimum-viability filter, not a guarantee the text is correct. Its job is
to supply plain speech so the model learns to leave correct text alone instead of
over-editing.

## The alignment check (all three pieces)

Every published row has its text force-aligned to its audio.

- Rows that do not align are dropped: 518 in total, 240 backbone and 278
  corrections, including the one broken 2.7 h timestamp.
- Rows that survive get a corrected span, `start_adj`/`end_adj` (the aligned words
  plus 0.2 s of padding). Cut training clips on those, not on the raw
  `start`/`end`: about a quarter of the raw spans clipped a word or caught a
  neighbour. Details in `data/hf-dataset/boundary-sync-report.md`.

## Vetting strength, at a glance

1. Hand-picked (4.71 h): reviewed per utterance by a person. Strongest.
2. NB2 leftover (8.91 h): filters, LLM judge, and a partial audio check.
3. Backbone (14.94 h): meeting-trust and alignment only.
4. Every row: force-aligned to audio and span-corrected.

## Split

80/20 train/val by hours, speaker-disjoint, so one speaker's audio never lands in
both. Validation is two whole held-out cities (orestiada, argos) plus whole seeded
speakers up to about 20%. A temporal test set (meetings from June 2026 on) is held
back entirely. The map and seed live in `split_assignments.json`.
