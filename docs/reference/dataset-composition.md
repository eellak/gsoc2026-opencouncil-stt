# Dataset composition & provenance

What the published Hugging Face dataset contains and **how each part was vetted**.
Generated by `eval/hf_export/` (spec [hf-dataset-export](../specs/hf-dataset-export.md));
live figures in `data/hf-dataset/stats.json`. Snapshot: 28.57 h, 36,846 rows,
metadata-only (audio fetched on demand — `scripts/fetch_clip.py`).

## The three pieces (28.57 h)

| Piece | rows | hours | train / val | how it was vetted |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Corrections — hand-picked** | 5,054 | **4.71 h** | 4.00 / 0.72 | full human review + explicit *include* |
| **Corrections — NB2 leftover** | 12,032 | **8.91 h** | 7.16 / 1.75 | automated pipeline (filters + LLM judge + partial audio) |
| **Backbone — no-edit** | 19,760 | **14.94 h** | 11.32 / 3.62 | meeting-trust + alignment |
| **Total** | 36,846 | **28.57 h** | 22.47 / 6.10 | + every row force-aligned to its audio |

Signal (corrections) **13.63 h (48%)** · plain speech (backbone) **14.94 h (52%)**.

## 1. Hand-picked corrections — 4.71 h (the strongest)

Utterances a reviewer personally opened in the review UI, saw the
`before → after` diff **and** the audio, made/approved the correction, and marked
**include** (worth training on). This is real per-utterance human review plus our
own inclusion decision — the highest-confidence part of the set.

(The raw included spans were 7.42 h; the alignment gate removed one utterance with
a broken timestamp — a 2.7-hour span from a data error — leaving 4.71 h of real
content.)

## 2. NB2 leftover corrections — 8.91 h (pipeline-vetted, not hand-picked)

Corrections chosen by the automated **"next-batch" (NB2)** selection pipeline —
*not* individually hand-picked by us. How NB2 was built (`data/next-batch/`):

1. **Start:** 93,584 candidate edits (every user/task edit in the corpus, minus
   already-curated ones).
2. **Hard prefilter** (duration / word-diff / edit-chain / CER-proxy bounds):
   → **61,730** survivors.
3. **Greedy-diverse shortlist** (balanced across error categories, clip lengths,
   cities): → **25,000**.
4. **LLM judge, 2 tiers** (keep / reject / unsure): → **13,377 "keep"**
   (rejected 8,953, unsure 2,636).
5. **Audio verification** of a city-balanced subset (5,011, cap 600/city, val
   cities excluded), re-transcribed and checked for faithfulness (`cer_local ≤
   0.2`): **4,329 confirmed faithful**.

**Who originally made these edits** (of the 12,032 we use): **26% human-made**
(`user_only`), **74% LLM-proposed then human-touched** (`task_then_user`), the
rest mixed. So they carry human signal but were *selected and vetted by pipeline,
not hand-reviewed one-by-one like piece 1*.

## 3. No-edit backbone — 14.94 h (plain speech)

Untouched ASR utterances from meetings a reviewer worked on but left **unchanged**.
Vetting: meeting-level trust (`frac_user ≥ 0.15` **or** the `humanReview` flag,
minus the denylist) + the alignment gate. This is *"alignment-passed no-edit
ASR"* — a minimum-viability filter, **not** individually verified-correct.
Purpose: plain speech so the model learns to **leave correct text alone** (avoids
the over-editing trap of a corrections-only set).

## The alignment check — applies to ALL three

Every published row's text is force-aligned to its audio:
- rows that don't align are **dropped** (518 total — 240 backbone + 278
  corrections; includes the one broken 2.7 h timestamp);
- surviving rows get a **corrected span** `start_adj`/`end_adj` (aligned words ±
  0.2 s) — **cut training clips on these, not the raw `start`/`end`** (≈¼ of the
  raw spans clipped a word or caught a neighbour). Detail:
  `data/hf-dataset/boundary-sync-report.md`.

## Vetting strength, at a glance

1. **Hand-picked (4.71 h)** — reviewed per-utterance by a person. Strongest.
2. **NB2 leftover (8.91 h)** — filters + LLM judge + partial audio check.
3. **Backbone (14.94 h)** — meeting-trust + alignment only.
4. **All rows** — force-aligned to audio, span-corrected.

## Split

80/20 train/val **by hours**, **speaker-disjoint** (one speaker's audio never
spans both). Validation = two whole held-out cities (orestiada, argos) + whole
seeded speakers to ~20%. A temporal test set (meetings from June 2026 on) is
withheld entirely. Map + seed: `split_assignments.json`.
